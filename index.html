Iron

The very first rank section in Valorant is Iron. Iron consists of three ranks: Iron I, Iron II, and Iron III. At Iron, many players may lack the basic fundamentals of a competitive shooter. This can include map awareness, spray control, and tap firing. Players may also not understand how each agent works and how to counter them with their own utility. Executing sites can end up being very sloppy as well. Players in Iron may not focus on their economy, and when a save round is needed. Unfortunately, lower rank sections like Iron tend to contain lots of smurfs, which are experienced players who create new accounts to deceive others. For all ranks, you gain more or less ranked rating depending on the score of the match and your performance.

  • Date: January 2020
  • Last Updated: 1/21/2021

Bronze

The second rank section in Valorant is Bronze. Bronze consists of three ranks: Bronze I, Bronze II, and Bronze III. At Bronze, players may still be lacking certain key components of the game, but overall have a basic understanding. Players in Bronze tend to lack team play and agent coordination. This includes baiting your teammates, or feeling nervous to peek and take a gunfight. Bronze is the last rank that can contain many players who are new to competitive shooters. For all ranks, you gain more or less ranked rating depending on the score of the match and your performance.

  • Date: January 2020
  • Last Updated: 1/21/2021

Silver

The third rank section in Valorant is Silver. Silver consists of three ranks: Silver I, Silver II, and Silver III. At Silver, players now have a good understanding of the game. This includes spray control and team play. You may see smarter economy plays like saving your gun for next round, or rushing into a site and risking a death to get a stronger financial benefit. Silver players also acknowledge agent synergy and trade off of teammate deaths. One important thing Silver players may lack is utility set up and over rotating. For all ranks, you gain more or less ranked rating depending on the score of the match and your performance.

  • Date: January 2020
  • Last Updated: 1/21/2021

Gold

The fourth rank section in Valorant is Gold. Gold consists of three ranks: Gold I, Gold II, and Gold III. Gold can tend to be a hard rank section to escape. This is due to lots of competition. Players will no longer over rotate, and now understand what most agents do and how to counter them. Site faking and split pushes are very prominent in Gold. Players now also understand important movement and shooting techniques like jiggle peeking and wall banging. For all ranks, you gain more or less ranked rating depending on the score of the match and your performance.

  • Date: January 2020
  • Last Updated: 1/21/2021

Platinum

The fifth rank section in Valorant is Platinum. Platinum consists of three ranks: Platinum I, Platinum II, and Platinum III. Platinum is known as the gateway rank. This is because it is the first rank section where things get serious. Platinum players have great team coordination, and tend to execute good setups on attacker or defender sides. Players at Platinum will no longer make mistakes that are common at lower ranks, and instead take full advantage of their agents abilities. For all ranks, you gain more or less ranked rating depending on the score of the match and your performance.

  • Date: January 2020
  • Last Updated: 1/21/2021

Diamond

The sixth rank section in Valorant is Diamond. Diamond consists of three ranks: Diamond I, Diamond II, and Diamond III. At Diamond, players exceed expectations. A Diamond players accuracy is exceptionally good, and agent synergy is almost always prominent. Players at this rank will always make sure their team comp is viable, like eliminating too many duelists and having the right amount of smokes. Diamond players do tend to make minor mistakes, like utility waste, but overall understand ranked Valorant at a high level. For all ranks, you gain more or less ranked rating depending on the score of the match and your performance.

  • Date: January 2020
  • Last Updated: 1/21/2021

Immortal

The second to last rank in Valorant is Immortal. Immortal consists of one rank and is ranked in the game leaderboard. At Immortal, you will expect semipro players and most of your top streamers, as well as upcoming players that are trying to make a name for themselves. In immortal you need to know how to play your agent to a tee. In this rank, you are only allowed to solo or duo queue to make the players focus on team play. The difference between Radiant and immortal is players that lack the team skill and or individual performance to go the extra mile. In Immortal there is little room for mistakes and if you aren't performing at your best you can easily find yourself deranked.

  • Date: January 2020
  • Last Updated: 2/5/2021

Radiant

The last rank section in Valorant is Radiant. Radiant does not have subranks within the rank itself. This rank is known for your top tier players like pros and streamers. To achieve this rank you need to climb the rank ladder, which is a hard task to accomplish for an everyday player. In radiant, you are only allowed to solo or duo queue. This is mainly because of the smaller player base at the higher rank. This forces players to have good team coordination and strategies on the fly. This is one of the reasons it's hard to stay radiant in Valorant. Most of the player base at the lower ranks likes to play as a five stack team for the best communication. Players at this rank also stay the same amount of elo(method for calculating the relative skill) regardless of how they play. This is good for players at radiant because of the common people that would focus on kills instead of the team aspect of winning the game.

  • Date: January 2020
  • Last Updated: 2/5/2021
